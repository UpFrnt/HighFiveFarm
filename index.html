Farm — Fresh — Local

High Five Farm is a two person team growing vegetables, herbs, and more on two leased acres in Woodford County. High Five produce is grown using non-certified organic methods, low-impact machinery and hand tools, locally sourced composts, and never sprayed with synthetic pesticides or amended with petro-chemical fertilizers. Farmers Chelsea and Alex are guided by their beliefs in simplicity, community, service, and ecological stewardship.

About Us

High Five Farm is operated by Chelsea 'Chelly' Barbour (she/her/hers) and Alex Rickman (he/him/his) , two lovebirds who started this farm business in 2020. Alex enjoys playing chess, cooking, and playing music. Chelly loves sending postcards, wildflower and tree identification, and tidying up. The name High Five celebrates teamwork, community cooperation, recognition of challenging physical work, and that good feeling after a job well done.



Most of our farming practices are non-mechanized and rely on work by hand, as we try to minimize the use of fossil fuels on the farm. We rely on cover crops, locally sourced horse manure compost, and hay for our soil fertility instead of petrochemical fertilizers. We try to disturb the soil as little as possible in order to maintain a high soil organic matter content, reduce erosion, and retain water more efficiently. We're working on transitioning our tunnel production to no-till. Floating row covers, insect netting, landscape fabric, and an intricate crop rotation are all utilized frequently to keep weeds and pests at bay instead of spraying insecticides and herbicides which inevitably will harm the ecosystem in and around the farm.

Join our spring/summer or fall season community supported agriculture program and receive a well-rounded weekly harvest of 6-10 items, grown by us, for you! CSA members are encouraged to try new things and are equipped with a recipe booklet designed for that week's offerings by Chef Toby Olsen. Connect with us through our weekly CSA member newsletter highlighting our growing practices, storage tips, and farmer check-ins.


Members of a CSA program are enthusiastically opting into the inherent risks involved in farming. Not every crop we attempt to grow will be a success. You will share in the failures and successes of the farm, thereby genuinely supporting local small agriculture.


Our CSA is available on a sliding scale, you will select which payment plan is most reasonable for you and your family. Learn more about our sliding scale CSA program and join today!
